## Why We Publish In Rockland County

Most people don't learn this until they're already filing: you publish in whichever county is listed as your PLLC's office location on the Articles of Organization. Not the county where you live. Not the county where you see clients.

New York's newspaper rules make publication cost depend entirely on that one county. A two-newspaper, six-week run in a high-cost county like Manhattan or the Bronx can run $1,000 or more. The same legal requirement costs a fraction of that in a lower-cost county.

We use our own registered address in Rockland County as the office location for every PLLC we form, no matter where you actually practice in New York. That's why we can quote one flat $885 price to a client in Manhattan and a client anywhere else in the state, instead of charging a different, often much higher, amount depending on your county.

A few things worth knowing:

- This is a legitimate, long-standing practice in New York LLC/PLLC formation. The statute requires a county of record for publication and service of process. It doesn't have to match your home or practice address.
- Your professional license and practice location aren't affected. Where your PLLC is licensed to practice and where its Articles list a registered office for publication are two different things.
- This isn't a publication service on its own, and you can't pick your own county through us. It's simply how we form every PLLC, so the flat price holds no matter where in New York you are. If you want to understand your own county's newspaper rules for other reasons, our [DIY formation guide](/how-to-form-a-pllc-in-ny) walks through that.

This section explains how the flat price works. It isn't a menu of publication options. If you're ready to move forward, the next step is [starting your formation](/order). We don't offer a standalone "just publish for me" service.

### Why cheap-looking quotes aren't the full price

Several national filing services advertise a low number, sometimes $99 or less, for the DOS filing step alone. Then they charge separately for publication, EIN, registered agent, and Operating Agreement drafting once you're further into checkout. Once you add up everything else, many clients find the "cheap" option costs at or above our flat $885, plus a second invoice later instead of one price up front.

## The Cost of Delay

NYSED pre-approval typically takes 8 to 12 weeks before you can even file with the Department of State, and publication itself runs six more consecutive weeks after filing. That timeline holds regardless of who handles your paperwork. A rejected OP packet, a mismatched name in a publication notice, or a missed newspaper week can each add weeks or months on top of it, and each restart usually means paying a fee again. If you're planning to open a practice on a specific date, starting early is the one thing you can control. There's no way to speed up NYSED's review itself.

**Why do you publish in Rockland County instead of my own county?**
New York law requires publication in the county listed as your PLLC's office location on its Articles of Organization, not necessarily where you live or practice. **Is DIY actually cheaper?**
Sometimes, on paper. Total state and newspaper fees alone can run a few hundred dollars less than $885 if your county's publication cost is low and nothing goes wrong. Most of our clients decide the time (several hours across 6+ weeks), the coordination between two state agencies and two newspapers, and the risk of paying twice after a rejection make the flat fee worth it.

**Do fees ever change after I've paid?**
No. Barring a specific optional add-on you request and approve in advance (like a DBA filing), $885 is the final price.

**Does this cost cover LLC formation too, or only PLLCs?**
This page covers PLLC formation specifically, for licensed professionals. We also form standard New York LLCs; see our [LLC formation page](/order-llc) for that pricing.

**What if I already have a PLLC formed in another state?**
Foreign qualification into New York is a different process with different pricing, since it depends on your home state's certified-document fees. See our [foreign PLLC qualification guide](/foreign-pllc). This cost breakdown is for new NY PLLC formation only.
