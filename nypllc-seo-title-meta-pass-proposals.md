## Top-line findings

1. **No missing metadata.** Every public page has a `title` + `description`. Good baseline hygiene.
2. **No literal duplicate titles.** Profession pages and foreign-state pages both follow a rigid template (`Form a New York {X} PLLC ... | NY PLLC Formation` / `{State} PLLC or PC Foreign Qualification in New York`), so titles differ only by the profession/state token. Not technically duplicates, but low differentiation — Google can still treat these as a "thin template" cluster if content thinness compounds it. Not currently a crisis; worth keeping an eye on as more state/profession pages are added.
3. **Descriptions are systematically too long — this is the single biggest fix.** Google's SERP snippet truncates at roughly 155–160 characters. Measured current lengths:
   - Homepage description: **215 chars** (should be ~150) — the back half of the sentence, including the *"Fast, simple, and compliant..."* value language, never renders.
   - DIY guide description: **224 chars**.
   - Profession-page descriptions average **~230 chars**, several (architect 283, engineer 278, chiropractor 232) run **80–130 characters over budget** — meaning **the `$885` price, the exact number this plan wants visible for conversion, is being cut off in the actual Google snippet on most profession pages today.** This is a real, measurable revenue-relevant bug, not just a style nit.
   - Virtual address, mail-forwarding-agreement, partners, contact, FAQ, about, order, order-llc: all **160–180+ chars**, all truncating.
   - Only the three legal boilerplate pages (privacy 133, terms 148, disclaimer 124) are within budget already.
4. **Titles are mostly within budget except profession pages.** Home/about/faq/contact/order/legal titles run 36–73 chars (fine). Profession-page titles run **44–93 chars** — the parenthetical alt-name (e.g. *"(Architecture & Landscape Architecture)"*, *"(Professional Engineer PLLC)"*) is what pushes several titles (architect 93, engineer 81, speech-language-pathologist 77, mhc 76) well past the ~60-char safe zone, so the tail end (often including the brand suffix) is truncated in the SERP today.
5. **Foreign-state pages (CT/FL/NJ/PA/TX) have no brand suffix at all** in their `<title>` — every other page ends in some form of "| NY PLLC Formation" / brand; these five are the only pages that don't. Minor brand-consistency gap, easy fix.
6. **Foreign-state pages price at $930–$1000, not $885** — this is correct/intentional (foreign qualification is priced per-state and is a different service than new-formation), but it means **the $885 headline price must never be pasted into these five pages or the hub.** Flagging this explicitly since the task brief's instruction to "include $885 where it helps conversion on money pages" does *not* apply here — doing so would be inaccurate pricing.
7. **Brand suffix is inconsistent site-wide:** shared `metadata.ts` pages (home/about/faq/contact/order/etc.) embed their own "|" already so the `generateMetadata()` helper's auto-suffix (`SEO_CONFIG.siteName` = "New York PLLC Formation Service") never actually fires; profession pages use "| NY PLLC Formation" (from `APP_CONFIG.name`); foreign-state pages use no suffix; the plan's own stated target pattern is the short "| NYPLLC". Proposals below standardize everyone on **"| NYPLLC"** (shorter → more room in the character budget, and matches the plan's stated pattern) — flagged as a house-style recommendation, not a hard requirement.
8. **Google Ads landing-page coordination risk — read before touching anything:**
   - **High risk (exact-match ad group targets today):** all 19 profession pages, the `/foreign-pllc` hub, and all 5 `/foreign-pllc/{state}` pages. Per the ads plan: *"One ad group per profession, each pointed at the live profession landing page"* and *"per-state exacts... each to its `/foreign-pllc/{state}` page."*
   - **Medium risk (sitelink extensions in the ads plan):** `/order`, `/how-to-form-a-pllc-in-ny`, `/virtual-address-services`, `/faq`, `/contact`, `/about`.
   - **Low/no risk:** `/order-llc`, `/partners`, `/privacy`, `/terms`, `/disclaimer`, `/mail-forwarding-agreement`.
   - **Title/meta-only changes (this pass) do not touch on-page ad-relevant copy, headlines, or CTAs**, so they carry lower QS risk than a body-copy rewrite — but any title/meta edit to the high-risk set should still be logged in the shared ads/CRO change log and not landed in the same window as an active bid-ladder step (per the ads plan's change-log rule).
